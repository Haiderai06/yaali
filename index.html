Ali a.s.


Bab-e Madinatul-'Ilm (Arabic: بَابِ مَديْنَةُ ٱلْعِلْم‎, "Door of City of the Knowledge")

About Imam Ali a.s.

  • Imam Ali a.s. was born inside the Kaaba in Mecca, the holiest place in Islam, to Abu Talib and Fatimah bint Asad.
  • Imam Ali a.s. ibn Abi Talib a.s. (Arabic: عَلِيّ ٱبْن أَبِي طَالِب‎, ʿAlī ibn ʾAbī Ṭālib; 13 September 601 – 29 January 661) was a cousin and son-in-law of the Islamic prophet Muhammad
  • Imam Ali a.s. was martyred in the Mosque of Kufa on 21st Ramadan while worshipping, 40 AH/661 CE, after being struck on the head with a sword by the Kharijite, Ibn Muljam l.a.

Imam Ali a.s. Titles

Ali is known by various titles, some given due to his personal qualities and others due to events in his life

  1. Al-Murtaza (Arabic: ٱلْمُرْتَضَىٰ‎, "The Chosen One")
  2. Amir al-Mu'minin (Arabic: أَمِير ٱلْمُؤْمِنِين‎, "Commander of the Faithful Ones")
  3. Bab-e Madinatul-'Ilm (Arabic: بَابِ مَديْنَةُ ٱلْعِلْم‎, "Door of City of the Knowledge")
  4. Abu Turab (Arabic: أَبُو تُرَاب‎, "Father of the Soil")
  5. Walad al-Kaʿbah (Arabic: وَلَد ٱلْکَعْبَة‎, "Son of the Kaaba")
